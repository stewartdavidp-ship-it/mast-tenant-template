Privacy Policy

Mast Platform · Effective March 29, 2026 · Last Updated March 29, 2026

1. Introduction

Mast Platform (“Mast,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) provides a hosted e-commerce platform designed for makers, artisans, and small businesses to sell their products online. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information when you interact with our platform.

This policy applies to:

  • runmast.com and all Mast-hosted tenant storefronts
  • The Mast admin application
  • All services provided through the Mast platform

2. Who This Policy Covers

This Privacy Policy covers three types of users:

  • Tenant Owners (“Merchants”) — Businesses and individuals who use Mast to create and manage their online storefronts.
  • Shoppers (“Customers”) — People who browse or purchase products from Mast-hosted storefronts.
  • Visitors — Anyone who visits runmast.com or a Mast-hosted storefront without making a purchase or creating an account.

If a Tenant Owner has published their own privacy policy for their storefront, that policy governs their relationship with their customers. However, this Mast Platform Privacy Policy continues to govern how Mast itself processes data on behalf of all users.

3. Information We Collect

Information You Provide Directly

Shoppers (during checkout and account interactions):

  • Name, email address, phone number
  • Shipping address (street, city, state, ZIP code)
  • Billing address (if different from shipping)
  • Commission request details (name, contact info, description of requested work)
  • Newsletter signup (email address, name)

Tenant Owners (during account setup and management):

  • Business name, contact name, email address, phone number
  • Account credentials (managed through Google Sign-In or email/password via Firebase Authentication)

Event Vendors (via vendor application forms):

  • Business name, contact name, email, phone, product category

Wholesale Customers:

  • Resale certificate number (in addition to standard checkout information)

Information Collected Automatically

When you visit any Mast-hosted storefront, we automatically collect:

  • IP address — collected via a third-party service (ipify.org) on each page view
  • Page views — which pages you visit and when
  • Interaction data — actions such as adding items to cart, clicking navigation links, or clicking social media links
  • Timestamps — date and time of each visit and interaction

We do not collect browser fingerprints, device identifiers, or user agent strings for tracking purposes.

Information From Third Parties

  • Payment processors (Square, Stripe) — Payment confirmation, transaction IDs, and payment status. We never receive or store your credit card number, CVV, or full payment card details.
  • Etsy — For Tenant Owners who connect their Etsy shop, we receive order data and listing information from the Etsy API (authorized by the Tenant Owner).
  • Plaid — For Tenant Owners who connect bank accounts for expense tracking, we receive transaction data (amounts, categories, merchant names, dates) via the Plaid API. This connection is initiated and authorized by the Tenant Owner.

4. How We Use Your Information

Service Delivery:

  • Processing and fulfilling orders (shipping, payment confirmation, order status updates)
  • Sending transactional emails (order confirmations, shipping notifications, commission proposals)
  • Sending SMS notifications (sale alerts, point-of-sale updates) when configured by the Tenant Owner
  • Managing Tenant Owner accounts and storefronts
  • Shipping label generation, address validation, and rate quotes

AI-Powered Features:

  • Generating social media content suggestions based on product data
  • Providing AI studio assistant responses based on business context and user prompts
  • Classifying product images using AI vision capabilities
  • Researching craft shows and events relevant to the Tenant Owner’s business

For more detail on how AI processes your data, see our AI Transparency Page.

Analytics:

  • Understanding how storefronts are used (page views, popular products, navigation patterns)
  • We use only our own built-in analytics. We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any third-party analytics or advertising services.

Communication:

  • Transactional emails related to orders and account activity
  • Newsletter communications (only with your consent, and you can unsubscribe at any time)
  • Event announcements for vendors and attendees

Payment Processing:

  • Facilitating payment through Square or Stripe hosted checkout pages
  • Managing platform subscription billing

6. AI and Machine Learning

Mast uses AI to power several features available to Tenant Owners. AI processes product data, business context, and user prompts to generate content, classify images, and provide research assistance.

Key points:

  • AI features are invoked only when a Tenant Owner actively uses them — there is no background AI processing of your data.
  • AI processing happens server-side through Mast’s backend. No data flows directly from your browser to the AI provider.
  • Our AI provider (Anthropic) does not use API data to train their models. Your data is processed and discarded, not retained for model improvement.

For complete details, including what data goes to AI, what does not, and how usage is metered, see our AI Transparency Page.

7. Data Storage and Security

  • Location: All data is stored in Google Cloud Platform (Firebase) infrastructure in the United States.
  • Encryption at rest: All stored data is encrypted using Google-managed encryption keys (AES-256).
  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between your browser and our servers is encrypted using TLS (HTTPS).
  • Payment data: Mast never stores credit card numbers, CVVs, or full payment card details. All payment processing is handled by Square or Stripe through their hosted checkout pages. Mast only stores transaction reference IDs.
  • Tenant isolation: Each Tenant Owner’s data is stored in an isolated path within our database, protected by security rules that prevent cross-tenant access.
  • Credentials and API keys: All sensitive credentials are stored in Google Cloud Secret Manager, not in application code or databases.

For more details, see our Security Statement.

9. Your Privacy Rights

Regardless of where you live, we provide all users with the following rights:

  • Right to Know — You can request a summary of the personal information we have about you.
  • Right to Delete — You can request that we delete your personal information.
  • Right to Correct — You can request that we correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to Opt Out — Although we do not sell personal information, you can contact us to opt out of any data processing you object to.

How to exercise your rights: Send an email to privacy@runmast.com with your request. We will acknowledge your request within 10 business days and respond substantively within 45 days. If we need additional time for complex requests, we will notify you of the extension. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

Breach notification: In the event of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify affected Tenant Owners within 72 hours of confirming the breach, and affected Shoppers as promptly as practicable. See our Security Statement for details on our incident response process.

10. California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA).

Categories of personal information we collect:

  • Identifiers (name, email, address, phone number, IP address)
  • Commercial information (order history, products purchased)
  • Internet activity (page views, interaction data)
  • Financial information (payment transaction IDs — not card numbers)

Your California rights:

  • Right to know what personal information we collect, use, and share
  • Right to delete your personal information
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information
  • Right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information — however, we do not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights

Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information: Mast does not sell personal information. We do not share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. No action is required on your part.

Sensitive Personal Information: Under the CPRA, certain categories of personal information receive enhanced protections. Mast may process sensitive personal information in connection with the Plaid bank account integration (financial account data), which is used solely to provide the expense tracking feature requested by the Tenant Owner. We do not use sensitive personal information for purposes beyond what is necessary to provide the requested service.

To exercise your California privacy rights, contact us at privacy@runmast.com.

11. Other US State Privacy Laws

We respect the privacy rights granted by state privacy laws across the United States, including those in Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Connecticut (CTDPA), Utah (UCPA), and other states that have enacted comprehensive privacy legislation.

If you are a resident of any of these states, you may exercise your rights as described in Section 9 of this policy. We apply the same privacy protections to all users regardless of location.

12. Children’s Privacy

Mast is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child under 13, we will take steps to delete that information promptly.

Mast does not provide age-gating controls on tenant storefronts. Tenant Owners whose products may appeal to children under 13 are responsible for implementing appropriate age verification and parental consent mechanisms as required by COPPA and applicable state laws.

If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us at privacy@runmast.com.
